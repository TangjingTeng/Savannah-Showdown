GAME DESIGN PROPOSAL

Mechanics

1. Basecamp and Resource Management

  • Players manage their ranger team, resources, and upgrades at the basecamp.
  • Resources like fuel, medical supplies, and food are required for survival and running patrols.
  • Players must allocate resources efficiently, deciding whether to strengthen defenses, upgrade medical facilities, or deploy teams, similar to Cities Skylines and Minecraft.

2. First-Person Exploration

  • Players take direct control in a first-person perspective to navigate the wilderness, using vehicles and tools to explore the environment, track poachers, and rescue animals.
  • The game includes an open-world system where players move between hotspots, finding new areas, wildlife, and threats.

3. Missions and Story Triggers

  • Dynamic Missions: Random events (e.g., poaching raids, animal rescues) triggered by the player's actions or in-game time, similar to how events unfold in GTA5.
  • Story-Based Missions: Major story arcs, such as uncovering a larger poaching syndicate, progressing the narrative. Missions can include stealth, combat, and strategic planning, like in Call of Duty and Men of War.

4. Combat and Patrol Engagements

  • Combat Mechanics: Players and their team engage poachers in firefights using non-lethal options depending on mission requirements.
  • Stealth Operations: Players can choose stealth, setting traps, or ambushing poachers to avoid detection, similar to Men of War's mechanics which the player needs to command the squad.

5. Wildlife Interaction

  • Animal Rescues: Players can rescue injured wildlife, treating them back at camp or delivering them to safe areas.
  • Tracking and Conserving: Players track endangered species, monitor their health, and ensure they are safe from poachers.

6. Consequences

  • Every decision (e.g., focusing too much on enforcement or ignoring local communities) affects the game's environment and story outcome. These decisions impact the health of the ecosystem and species.

Story

The game is structured around a core storyline that unravels as players explore the environment and tackle poaching incidents. The game is divided into three main stages:

1. Early Stage (Introduction)

  • Players arrive as the new head ranger and learn about basic survival, conservation efforts, and the initial threat from small-time poachers. Early missions focus on resource gathering, patrolling, and learning the mechanics.
  • Example Mission: Rescue a baby rhino after its mother has been poached, highlighting the emotional toll of the trade.

2. Mid Stage (Conflict Escalation)

  • As players progress, larger, more organized poaching operations emerge. The player must balance patrolling, rescuing animals, and strengthening relationships with local communities to build alliances.
  • Example Mission: Uncovering an illegal ivory trade ring that spans the region.

3. End Stage (Climactic Resolution)

  • The player must take down a major poaching syndicate, using everything learned throughout the game, including tactical combat, animal protection, and strategic decision-making.
  • Example Mission: A final mission involving a large-scale poaching bust, saving a species on the brink of extinction.

Aesthetics

1. Visual Style

  • Natural Realism: Detailed, immersive natural environments filled with lush forests, wildlife, and changing weather patterns. The aesthetic inspiration comes from games like The Last of Us and SimCry, with environments that shift based on the player's actions.
  • Animal Models: Realistic representations of endangered species, highlighting their beauty and fragility.
  • Night/Day Cycle: The environment changes dynamically, creating different gameplay experiences depending on the time of day (e.g., patrolling at night is more dangerous).

2. Sound

  • Ambient Sound: The game features authentic nature sounds like wind, animal calls, rustling leaves, and water. The sound design enhances immersion by using environmental cues to signal danger (e.g., the distant sound of poachers or injured animals).
  • Music: Epic, orchestral music for major story moments, with softer, atmospheric tones during exploration.

3. Haptic Feedback

  • Players experience haptic feedback when engaging in combat or interacting with animals, adding a layer of immersion through vibration when firing weapons or treating injured wildlife.

Technology

1. Game Engine

  • The game will be built using Unreal Engine 5 or Unity for its advanced rendering capabilities and ability to create vast, dynamic environments with high levels of detail, especially for wildlife and nature.
  • AI System: Complex AI drives both wildlife behaviors (e.g., migration, survival instincts) and poacher activity, creating an evolving challenge for players.

2. Platform

  • The game will be available on PC and next-gen consoles (PlayStation 5, Xbox Series X), ensuring powerful processing capabilities to handle the large open-world environment, detailed models, and dynamic systems.

3. Assets

  • 3D Models: High-quality assets for animals, vehicles, and environment features, developed with realistic textures and animations.
  • Dynamic Weather System: Built using advanced environmental tools to simulate real-time weather changes that affect gameplay, like rainstorms, fog, or droughts.

User Testing & Feedback

Testing Process

During the first user-testing session in Unity, players were tasked with exploring the game environment, completing a basic mission, and interacting with the UI. The focus was on evaluating the aesthetic realism of the savannah terrain and the functionality of the movement controls.

Key Feedback

Terrain Realism

  • Players felt that the terrain lacked the distinct features of a savannah environment.
  • Suggestions included adding more realistic elements like sparse vegetation, acacia trees, and a more dynamic color palette.

UI Movement Controls

  • Testers commented that the UI for movement resembled a mobile game joystick.
  • Many users suggested relying solely on keyboard and mouse controls for a smoother experience.

Adjustments Made

Enhanced Terrain

  • Added scattered trees, taller grasses, and rocky outcrops
  • Adjusted lighting and textures for authentic savannah tones

Streamlined Controls

  • Implemented intuitive keyboard and mouse controls
  • Simplified HUD for essential elements only
